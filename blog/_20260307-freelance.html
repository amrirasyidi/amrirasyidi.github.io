It's been a while since I started to pick up more project on upwork and I feel like to reflect on the ...

I know upwork since a long time ago, back when not everybody will be accepted to start a side gigs there. I know it because I was one of that person. Back in 2020 I saw a linkedin profile that list upwork in their professional experience, out of curiosity I check the website and decided to sign up. At that time, I only have roughly 2 years of experience as a professional. After filling some forms to sign up, I waited for some sort of approval/confirmation, and upon receiving it I was noticed that I'm not accepted. Imagine applying for a side job and got declined lol.

Fast forward to now (by February or March roughly), I found again some linkedin profiles that mentioned upwork as their professional experience, some even wrote a post about their upwork's projects and achievements, then I got reminded, "hey, I tried applying for upwork before, let me revisit!".

Surprisingly (or not, because I didn't even have a chance to work on upwork before), I can "revive" my account and I got quite a lot of connect (this is a currency in upwork), and I can apply to the posted jobs on upwork. I haven't really check the most updated t n c, but I don't think there are restriction on who can sign up and apply to work on a project on upwork (please correct me if I'm wrong, but I'm not really in the mood for fact checking lol).

Got excited because of the chance to work on a side gig, I applied and used my connect carelessly and before I knew it, I already running low on my connects. And this is where the first lessons come.

1. Connect is a limited resource, use it wisely.

If you are applying to an upwork project. Some connects will be required, take it as some capital you need to start a business (or a project really). Look for a promising client:

  • Payment verified badge
  • Have a good review
  • Have spent some money on upwork

Maximize search

2. Think about 0 to 1

If you just starting, the first thing you should aim is to have a completed upwork project. This has 2 benefit at least for me. One, this will serve as my upwork portfolio, showcasing that I was able to finish a project successfully. Second, and this is actually more important, it gives you a sense of achievement, and it can snowball your motivation to keep working! Look for a project with higher chance

  • Find something you can finish quickly
  • Don't think too much about the budget
  • Few proposal
  • Freshly posted
