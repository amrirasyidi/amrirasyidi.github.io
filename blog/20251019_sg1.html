That One Time I Went to Singapore

Published on August 13rd, 2025

Talking points

  1. How I feel about the city/country
    1. Tidy and organized (obviously).
    2. Compared to Indonesia.
      1. the roads are larger
      2. fewer private car
      3. more bus--they only have 2 modes of public transport (bus and MRT) afaik
      4. transits and routes make a lot of sense
    3. walking is very pleasant, despite the hot climate.
    4. on the second day, we walked 22k steps
    5. I don't see any beggar at all.
    6. prices are over the ceiling.
    7. there are quite a lot of mosque as well, it is easy to pray there.
    8. Felt bland.
      this might be biased since we are mainly only visit bookstores, I mean there is USS and shits there
  2. The TV show
    1. They doesn't seem to have a very serious problem.
    2. They are "broadcasting" problems in other regions.
      Japan's conflict (Ryukyu).
    3. And then at some point, they are broadcasting the literal rat problem (and they took it seriously).

Should I make it chronological? Nah, let's just make it my opinion.

Back in late September, my wife and I went for a vacation, a long-awaited vacation. I "finally" can make the time since this also marks my transition to a new job at a new company. I got 2 weeks "off" and my wife works remotely now.

We've already got several places on our wishlist, considering the budget and timing, we decided Singapore is an option we can manage in such a short period of preparation. It's not that far (accessibility wise) yet far enough the ambient with Indonesia, we don't have to worry about visa, we can also still make the budget.

City tour is not really my thing, as in, I would just literally walk around, probably missing a lot (of time and money) if I'm in charge of the itinerary. Luckily, my wife has an "ambition" to visit as many bookstores and library as possible, so she prepared every point of the itinerary: which bookstores to visit and where we were going to eat.

The rest of the plan was just to book the hotel and the flight. Since many things can be paid with card there, we also prepared our card.

The second my feet touched this advanced land, I can already felt the "stark" differences with my home country. Everything just make sense here, the signs really helped a lot, we didn't even communicate once with the locals here to find things, just consult with the signs.

This "feeling" will be the main point of my blog post, so if you are expecting a chronological story, you wont find it here.

My first impression, besides of how helpful the signs are, is that this city is very clean, tidy, and organized.
